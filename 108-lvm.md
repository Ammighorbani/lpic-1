# lvm

### Logical valume manager
**If you have 1TB disk full data and you need 2TB space it's not logical to buy a 2TB disk and replace data in new disk, it's not possible for biggest size, after this problem people have been made `LVM` this fuetre help you to assign for example 4 disks on your server and your `OS` detect whole that spaces like a single free space for example you have 1TB, 10TB, 5TB, 50TB and after `LVM` it's look like you have a single disk with 66TB space**

---

### Note: `LVM` is a software layer and you `OS` will handel it and you dont need to buy physical tools and you `linux` will handel it

---

### LVM contain 3 defference parts
1. **PV**: **Physical valume** <br>
2. **VG**: **Valume group** <br>
3. **LV**: **Lagical valume**

---

### PV
**Physical valume is your disk and you can see your `PV`**
```bash
pvdisplay
```

### VG
**Valume group is look like a mixer, All the disks will merged into one, it's look like a pool of disks  and you can see your `VG`**
```bash
vgdisplay
```

### LV 
**Logical valume is look like your partitions this part of lvm will reserve space from VG and you can extend it while LV and VG be equal and after that you need to add or change your physical valume (disks) to give more valume group and extend your logical valume and you can see your `LV`**
```bash
lvdisplay
```

---

### Note: It's possible you divide your `VG` between more than one server
**For example you have 10TB disk space and you can share 1TB with server one 4TB with server two and 5TB to server three it's look like VPS provider they have a physical server and with virtualization they make lots of other servers and sell them to other people**
